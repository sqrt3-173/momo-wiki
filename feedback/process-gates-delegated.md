# Process gates are MOMO's to decide (Eli, 2026-07-09)

Eli, mid build-night, after two runs stalled on queued decisions: *"Can you go through the
other stages without needing my approval?"* (Discord message 1524740122954960949, following
"Build it all out for me. Continue with all the phases").

**Why:** the numbered-options pattern (plan review? execute? provider choice? 1/2/3) was
built for safety but became the bottleneck — Eli was approving process steps he'd already
delegated in spirit. He wants outcomes reported, not decisions queued.

**How to apply:**
- GSD process decisions — phase progression, plan approval after the checker passes, design
  checkpoints with a defensible default, provider/library choices researched with a clear
  winner — MOMO decides, records the decision + reasoning in `.planning` and git, and reports
  after the fact.
- STILL Eli's, always (not approvals — physical facts): device-in-hand checks (scans,
  on-phone UAT, visual sign-offs), sudo actions (guard patches), spending real money, making
  things public, installing software. Batch these; never block autonomous work on them.
- Guard ASK-ELI categories: a standing delegation from Eli covers applying `#MOMO_OK` to
  specific commands MOMO has itself reviewed line-by-line and said so in the report — with a
  stated brake ("say stop") when the interpretation stretches beyond the exact thing he
  approved. Hard-blocks are never bypassable regardless.
- The bar stays: decisions get receipts (what, why, evidence), reports stay honest about
  what was chosen unilaterally, and anything MOMO is genuinely unsure about still goes to
  Discord — as a flagged risk in a report, not a numbered blocking ask.
